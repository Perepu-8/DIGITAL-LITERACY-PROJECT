# Case Study: MobiKwik UPI Payment Fraud (2025)

**Crime Type:** Online Payment Fraud / UPI Fraud

---

## Description

In September 2025, Indian fintech company MobiKwik experienced one of the largest digital payment frauds in the country's history. The cyberattack exploited a critical vulnerability in the company's payment system following a recent software update. Over a 48-hour period on September 11 and 12, fraudsters executed fraudulent transactions totalling approximately Rs 40 crore (€4.3 million), draining funds from the company's accounts.

---

## Step-by-Step Execution

1. **Exploitation of System Flaw**  
   Fraudsters identified a vulnerability introduced during a software update that allowed UPI transactions exceeding available wallet balances.

2. **Bypassing Security**  
   In multiple cases, the system accepted incorrect UPI PINs—a fundamental security failure.

3. **Undetected Activity**  
   The suspicious outflows remained unnoticed for two days until a routine audit on September 13.

4. **Arrests**  
   Six individuals were later arrested, with investigators suspecting an inside job due to the sophisticated nature of the attack.

---

## Targets and Consequences

While the company was the primary victim, over 5 lakh (500,000) fraudulent transactions affected individual users. Consequences included:

- **Financial Loss** – Rs 40 crore drained; only Rs 14 crore recovered
- **Regulatory Scrutiny** – Exposed weak fraud detection systems
- **Reputational Damage** – MobiKwik's second major security breach, eroding consumer trust

This case demonstrates how inadequate monitoring and insider threats enable large-scale online payment fraud.
