# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

Cybercrime is on the rise, especially among young adults who are active on digital platforms. Follow this checklist to protect yourself from online threats.

---

## General Prevention Tips

### 1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords
Create complex passwords using a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms. Use a password manager to store them securely.

### 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Activate 2FA on all important accounts such as email, social media, banking, and payment apps. This adds an extra layer of security even if your password is compromised.

### 3. Be Wary of Unsolicited Messages
Do not click on links or download attachments from unknown senders on WhatsApp, Telegram, SMS, or email. Fraudsters often pose as friends, companies, or government officials.

### 4. Verify Before Sharing Personal Information
Never share your Aadhaar number, PAN, passport details, or sensitive photos with unverified websites or individuals. Scammers often use social engineering to extract personal data.

### 5. Keep Devices and Apps Updated
Regularly update your smartphone, laptop, and applications. Updates often contain critical security patches that protect against known vulnerabilities.

### 6. Review App Permissions
Check what permissions you have granted to apps on your phone. Avoid giving unnecessary access to contacts, SMS, or location to apps that do not require them.

---

## UPI / Financial Safety Tips

### 7. Never Share UPI PIN, OTP, or Card Details
No bank representative, customer support agent, or government official will ever ask for your UPI PIN, OTP, or debit/credit card details. Treat any such request as a scam immediately.

### 8. Use UPI Apps with Caution
- Always check the payee name before confirming any payment
- Enable transaction alerts via SMS and email
- Set daily transaction limits on your bank account
- Never scan QR codes from unknown or untrusted sources

---

## Reporting Mechanism

If you become a victim of cybercrime or notice suspicious activity:

| **Method** | **Details** |
|------------|-------------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Cyber Helpline Number** | **1930** (24x7 toll-free) |

Immediately report any financial fraud to **1930** to block fraudulent transactions and initiate the complaint process.
